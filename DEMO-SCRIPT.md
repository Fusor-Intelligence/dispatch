# Dispatch — 2-Minute Demo Script

## Opening (10 seconds)

> "Support inboxes are black holes. Tickets pile up, patterns get buried, and your team spends most of their time on problems AI could handle. Dispatch changes that."

---

## Screen 1: Deploy (20 seconds)

*Start on the Deploy screen. The Dispatch logo and mission presets are visible.*

> "Dispatch isn't a dashboard you configure — it's an operator you assign."

*Click each mission preset as you say them:*

> "Tell it what you care about: reduce workload, auto-reply to repetitive requests, escalate anything risky, surface product issues before they snowball."

*Gesture at the guardrails:*

> "Set your safety rails — nothing sends without your approval — and hit Start Sweep."

*Click **Start Sweep**.*

---

## Screen 2: Live Sweep (20 seconds)

*The sweep screen animates through each step. Let it run.*

> "Now Dispatch goes to work. It's reading every conversation in your inbox, classifying them by category and urgency, drafting responses for the ones it can handle, and clustering the rest into patterns."

*As steps complete:*

> "This isn't a loading screen — it's doing real analysis. Every email gets a category, a sentiment read, a confidence score, and a routing decision."

*It auto-transitions to the Brief screen.*

---

## Screen 3: Agent Brief (25 seconds)

*The narrative summary appears with the key numbers.*

> "And here's the payoff. Instead of opening your inbox to 47 unread messages, you get a briefing."

*Point to the numbers:*

> "31 emails handled automatically. 9 need your judgment. And 8 are tied to the same checkout bug your users keep hitting."

*Point to the hero cluster card:*

> "Dispatch found the pattern on its own — 8 separate complaints, all the same root cause, severity critical. It's already recommending you escalate to engineering."

> "You didn't triage a single ticket to get here."

*Click **Set Rules**.*

---

## Screen 4: Rules of Engagement (20 seconds)

> "Now you teach it how aggressive to be."

*Drag the confidence slider:*

> "Set the threshold — anything above 90% confidence ships without review. Below that, it waits for you."

*Toggle a rule:*

> "Always escalate angry complaints. Route critical bugs straight to engineering. Require review on cancellations."

*Tap a tone option:*

> "Even pick the tone — calm, apologetic, concise, or white-glove premium."

> "You're not managing tickets. You're setting policy."

*Click **Activate Dispatch**.*

---

## Screen 5: Command Deck (25 seconds)

*The Command Deck loads with queue panels and email list.*

> "This is your command deck. Dispatch is running support ops — you're supervising the edge cases."

*Point to the queue panels:*

> "7 ready to send, 6 need your review, 4 escalations, 3 active incidents. That's your entire inbox, triaged in seconds."

*Click an email row to expand the detail panel:*

> "Click into any case — you see the original email, the AI analysis, and the draft reply. One click to approve, escalate, or override."

*Click the Incidents queue:*

> "And incidents aren't tags — they're cases. Dispatch groups the complaints, tracks the trend, and tells you what to do about it."

---

## Close (10 seconds)

> "Most support tools make you process tickets. Dispatch makes you the decision-maker. You set the mission, get a briefing, define the rules, and supervise the outcome."

> "That's Dispatch. AI support ops, not another inbox."
